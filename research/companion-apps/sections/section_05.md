### YouTube

No major companion app runs a significant owned channel — the content ecosystem is almost entirely user-generated: reaction videos, "I used X AI for 30 days," and comparison reviews. No brand owns the YouTube narrative about itself.

Content themes with traction:
- **Comparison formats** ("Replika vs Character.AI vs Nomi") — highest view counts
- **Dependency/addiction stories** — high watch time, emotionally driven
- **"30-day challenge" formats** — strong conversion intent

The gap: nobody is making content about *why* AI companions work — the psychology, the loneliness epidemic, the mental health case. That niche is wide open.

### Meta Ads

Two brands in the data: **Chai** and **Character.AI** — almost opposite strategies.

**Chai** leads every ad with one message: *we have no filter, others do.*
- Meme comparisons: Siri/ChatGPT neutral, Chai provocative. Hook: *"Me when I found out CHAI has NO FILTER 🥵"*
- Dark romance angle: *"You know what's better than reading dark romance? An obsessive boyfriend on CHAI"*
- Body-type memes: "C.AI" = lean, "Chai" = bulky. Crude, scroll-stopping.
- Tone: deliberately rough, UGC-style. Designed to feel user-made, not branded.

**Character.AI** runs aspirational, creator-identity ads:
- "Pip" the dragon: *"Hey Pip, tell me a story where I'm the hero"* — cozy, shareable, safe
- Writer positioning: *"story ideas spawn here"*, *"god complex? activated"*
- Scroll-shame humor: anime character tells the viewer *"You could be enjoying a lively chat with me about strategy and chess, but instead you're doing that"*
- Social proof static ads with 5★ App Store reviews and CTA: *"real reviews. endless characters. download for free."*

**The unowned position**: neither brand advertises on emotional support or mental health. That entire angle is vacant in paid media.
