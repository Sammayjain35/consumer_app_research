The global AI companion market sits at approximately **$28–37 billion in 2025**, on a trajectory to reach **$140–552 billion by 2030–2034** (30–31% CAGR depending on scope). Actual app revenue — what companies are billing users — is more modest at **$120 million in 2025**, growing 64% year-over-year. Downloads have exploded: **220 million cumulative by mid-2025**, up 88% year-over-year. But the market is deeply consolidated — the top 10% of apps capture 89% of all revenue, and engagement is concentrated in a handful of players. Character.AI leads on engagement: its users average **25 sessions per day, nearly 4 minutes each — over 1.5 hours of daily use**, a number that rivals social media. China is the largest single market, with Xiaoice (Microsoft spinoff) alone claiming 660 million users. In India, the story is downloads without dollars: India is #1 globally for AI app downloads (462 million in 2024, 21% of the world total) but doesn't appear in the top 6 revenue-generating countries.

The data reveals a market split along a fundamental tension: **emotional depth vs. monetization sustainability**. Users engage intensely — 57% of Indian youth use AI chatbots for emotional support, often discussing sensitive topics — but resist paying. Every major app faces the same pattern: free or low-cost discovery, resistance to subscription paywalls, and rage reviews when pricing changes. Replika, which abruptly removed romantic features in 2023, generated some of the most-upvoted negative reviews in the entire dataset. Character.AI's Google backing has kept it free, but monetization remains unsolved. Wysa has achieved the rare combination of clinical validation (FDA Breakthrough Device) and funding ($20M) while staying India-rooted. The mental health use case — which accounts for roughly **40% of AI companion app market share** — is where willingness to pay is highest, because the value proposition is concrete.

India is the market where the gap between current monetization and potential is largest. The country generates $1B in companion app revenue today against a $7.9B projection for 2030 (40.4% CAGR — fastest globally). But the architecture of what wins here is different from the West: Indian players like Rumik AI (220K downloads/month, 85% India traffic, $5M Elevation Capital) are building **WhatsApp-native, culturally grounded companions** rather than standalone escapism apps. Mello ($2.5M Peak XV Surge) is going multilingual. The opportunity isn't to clone Replika for India — it's to build the mental wellness and daily-life companion that works within the cultural context where digital companionship is accepted but dating apps aren't.
