### Major Players

**Replika** built the largest emotionally-attached user base in the West — 40M+ users, 85% report emotional connections, 60% of paying users in romantic relationships. Its fatal mistake was the February 2023 feature removal of erotic talk, later partially reversed. The highest-upvoted review in the dataset (208 helpful) is a user documenting their disillusionment through multiple rating updates — a trust-destruction arc playing out slowly. A "New Replika" with a more advanced LLM is reportedly planned.

**Character.AI** dominates on sheer engagement — 185M monthly visits, 25 sessions/day per user. But the highest-upvoted review (655 helpful) reads: *"you've effectively ruined what was a nice little app"* — a sign that aggressive moderation and quality degradation have hurt its core base. MAU fell from 28M peak to 20M. The November 2025 under-18 lockdown (redirecting minors to a restricted "Stories" mode) removed a significant share of its most-engaged users.

**Wysa** has the strongest clinical credibility of any app in this set — FDA Breakthrough Device designation, deployed across NHS serving 300K+ patients with 95% diagnostic accuracy. But its Play Store reviews reveal a perception problem: users feel they're interacting with a scripted button tree, not real AI. The data says the AI works; the UX says it doesn't feel like AI. The March 2025 acquisition of April Health and the June 2025 US launch of Wysa Gateway signal a pivot toward B2B health system integration over direct-to-consumer.

**Rumik AI** is the most interesting India-native competitor. 350 helpful upvotes on a 1★ review about a voice call paywall — the highest negative signal of any Indian app in the dataset — suggests it has an engaged, paying-willing user base that was burned by a pricing change. WhatsApp-native architecture is a structural differentiator that no Western player has replicated.

**Woebot** has the money ($123M) and the clinical framework but a fundamental UX problem: its crisis detection is calibrated so sensitively that normal idioms like "put out the fire" trigger crisis protocols. Users describe it as frustrating rather than helpful when they need it most.

### Minor Players

**Anima AI**, **Kindroid**, and **Kuki** serve niche segments (romantic, customizable, and casual respectively) without breakout scale. **YourDOST** (₹100 Cr funding) and **Mello** ($2.5M Peak XV Surge) are the India-specific minor players worth watching — both are pivoting toward professional mental health integration rather than pure companionship.
