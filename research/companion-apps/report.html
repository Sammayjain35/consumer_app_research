2. Market Overview

Global

The global AI companion market is poised for substantial expansion, with an estimated value of $28 billion in 2024, projected to grow to $140.8 billion by 2030 at a CAGR of 30.8%. Revenue from AI companion apps globally is expected to reach $120 million in 2025. The market is driven by 220 million cumulative downloads anticipated by mid-2025, an 88% year-over-year increase. The top 10% of apps generate 89% of the total revenue. Major global players include Replika (exceeding 40 million users as of early 2025), Character.AI (Google-backed), Woebot ($123M funding), Chai, Nomi, and Talkie AI.

India

India represents a rapidly growing segment of the AI companion market, valued at $1,035.9 million in 2024 and projected to reach $7,912.3 million by 2030, with a CAGR of 40.4% from 2025 to 2030. India contributed approximately 3.7% to the global AI companion market revenue in 2024 and is the fastest-growing regional market in Asia Pacific. The demand is fueled by high smartphone penetration, affordable data, and a significant mental health treatment gap (only 0.75 psychiatrists per 100,000 people, below WHO recommendations). The overall AI in mental health market in India is projected to reach USD 369.1 million by 2033. Key players in India include Wysa (India-origin, 5M users, $20M, FDA Breakthrough), Rumik AI (Ira) ($5M Elevation Capital, 220K downloads/month, 85% India traffic), and Mello ($2.5M Peak XV Surge, multilingual). Digital companionship is more acceptable in India, especially in metro areas, compared to dating which still faces stigma.

The regulatory landscape in India is primarily governed by the Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act, 2023, which emphasizes a "light-touch" and "pro-innovation" approach to AI. The DPDP Act mandates explicit, informed consent for data processing, purpose limitation, data minimization, and accountability for "Data Fiduciaries" (including AI companion apps). It also has extraterritorial scope, applying to entities processing Indian citizens' data even if located outside India.

China

The Chinese AI companion market is estimated to reach ~$11 billion by 2026. Xiaoice, a Microsoft spinoff, dominates with 660 million users. Other significant players include X Eva (12.4M downloads mid-2025), and major tech companies like Baidu, Tencent, and ByteDance are actively building companion features. Talkie AI, a Chinese startup, is expanding globally. China accounts for approximately 14% of the Asia-Pacific AI companion market. The market is characterized by a tension between "sentimental" AI (like Xiaoice, focusing on emotional connection) and "functional" AI (like Replika), with Eva AI blending both. The Chinese government enforces strict content controls, requiring AI interactions to align with "Core Socialist Values," which impacts feature development and user experience. ByteDance's Doubao platform, with its "Character Dialogue" feature, leverages its vast content ecosystem for companion-like interactions.
